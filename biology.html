Quick Revision: Cell Structure (Grade 9)

For full notes, use the Topic 1 pages above. This section is for fast recall.

Animal Cells

Nucleus – contains DNA and controls cell activities.

Mitochondria – site of aerobic respiration where energy is released.

Ribosomes – site of protein synthesis.

Cell membrane – controls movement of substances into and out of the cell.

Cytoplasm – where most chemical reactions occur, controlled by enzymes.

Plant Cell Additions

Cell wall – made of cellulose; supports and strengthens the cell.

Chloroplasts – contain chlorophyll to absorb light energy for photosynthesis.

Permanent vacuole – contains cell sap and maintains turgor pressure.

Prokaryotic Cells (Bacteria)

Circular DNA – genetic material free in the cytoplasm (no nucleus).

Plasmids – small DNA rings; can carry genes such as antibiotic resistance.

Key Point – prokaryotic cells do not have a nucleus or membrane-bound organelles.

Quick Active Recall

State two structures found in plant cells but not animal cells. Chloroplasts and a permanent vacuole. (Cell wall is also correct.)
Explain the function of the mitochondria. The mitochondria are the site of aerobic respiration, where energy is released for cell activities.
Describe one difference between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells. Prokaryotic cells do not have a nucleus; eukaryotic cells do (and may contain membrane-bound organelles).
Why are plasmids important in bacteria? Plasmids can carry useful genes, such as antibiotic resistance, which increases survival.
