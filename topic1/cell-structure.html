Cell Structure

This page covers animal, plant, and prokaryotic cells at AQA GCSE Higher (Grade 9) level.

Animal Cells

Structure Function
Nucleus Contains DNA and controls the activities of the cell.
Mitochondria Site of aerobic respiration, where energy is released.
Ribosomes Site of protein synthesis.
Cell membrane Controls the movement of substances into and out of the cell.
Cytoplasm Where most chemical reactions occur, controlled by enzymes.

Plant Cells

Plant cells contain all the structures of animal cells plus:

Structure Function
Cell wall Made of cellulose; strengthens the cell and supports its shape.
Chloroplasts Contain chlorophyll to absorb light energy for photosynthesis.
Permanent vacuole Contains cell sap and maintains turgor pressure, keeping the cell rigid.

Prokaryotic Cells (Bacterial Cells)

Structure Function
Cell wall Supports the cell; not made of cellulose.
Cell membrane Controls movement of substances into and out of the cell.
Cytoplasm Where chemical reactions take place.
Circular DNA Genetic material free in the cytoplasm; not enclosed in a nucleus.
Plasmids Small rings of DNA that can carry genes such as antibiotic resistance.
Ribosomes Smaller than eukaryotic ribosomes; site of protein synthesis.

Key point: Prokaryotic cells do not have a nucleus or membrane-bound organelles.

Grade 9 Comparisons

Compare prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells (4 marks) Eukaryotic cells have a nucleus and membrane-bound organelles, whereas prokaryotic cells do not. Prokaryotic DNA is circular and free in the cytoplasm, while eukaryotic DNA is linear and enclosed in a nucleus.

6-Mark Model Answer

Describe the differences between animal, plant, and bacterial cells. Animal and plant cells are eukaryotic and contain a nucleus, mitochondria, ribosomes, cytoplasm, and a cell membrane. Plant cells also contain chloroplasts for photosynthesis, a cellulose cell wall for support, and a permanent vacuole to maintain turgor pressure. Bacterial cells are prokaryotic and do not have a nucleus or membrane-bound organelles. Their genetic material is circular and found free in the cytoplasm, and they may contain plasmids.

Active Recall Questions

Which structures are unique to plant cells? Cell wall, chloroplasts, and a permanent vacuole.
What is the function of mitochondria? Site of aerobic respiration where energy is released.
Why are plasmids important in bacteria? They can carry genes such as antibiotic resistance, increasing survival.
