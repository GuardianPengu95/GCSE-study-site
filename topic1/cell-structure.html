Cell Structure

AQA GCSE Biology Higher — complete specification coverage for animal, plant, and prokaryotic cells.

Animal Cells

Structure Function (Exam-Ready)
Nucleus Contains genetic material (DNA) and controls the activities of the cell.
Mitochondria Site of aerobic respiration, where energy is released.
Ribosomes Site of protein synthesis.
Cell membrane Controls the movement of substances into and out of the cell.
Cytoplasm Where most chemical reactions take place, controlled by enzymes.

Plant Cells

Plant cells contain all the structures of animal cells plus the following:

Structure Function (Exam-Ready)
Cell wall Made of cellulose; strengthens the cell and supports its shape.
Chloroplasts Contain chlorophyll to absorb light energy for photosynthesis.
Permanent vacuole Contains cell sap and maintains turgor pressure, keeping the cell rigid.

Prokaryotic Cells (Bacterial Cells)

Structure Function (Exam-Ready)
Cell wall Supports the cell; not made of cellulose.
Cell membrane Controls movement of substances into and out of the cell.
Cytoplasm Where chemical reactions take place.
Circular DNA Genetic material that controls the cell; not enclosed in a nucleus.
Plasmids Small rings of DNA that may carry genes such as antibiotic resistance.
Ribosomes Smaller than eukaryotic ribosomes; site of protein synthesis.

Key specification point: Prokaryotic cells do not have a nucleus or membrane-bound organelles.

Comparison: Animal, Plant, and Prokaryotic Cells

Feature Animal Plant Prokaryotic
Nucleus Yes Yes No
Mitochondria Yes Yes No
Cell wall No Yes (cellulose) Yes (not cellulose)
Chloroplasts No Yes No

6-Mark Model Answer

Describe the differences between animal, plant, and bacterial cells. Animal and plant cells are eukaryotic and contain a nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, mitochondria, and ribosomes. Plant cells also contain chloroplasts for photosynthesis, a cellulose cell wall for support, and a permanent vacuole to maintain turgor pressure. Bacterial cells are prokaryotic and do not have a nucleus or membrane-bound organelles. Their genetic material is circular and found free in the cytoplasm, and they may contain plasmids.
