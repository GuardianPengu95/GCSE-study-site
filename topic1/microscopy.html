Microscopy

Grade 9 focus: use correct terms (magnification vs resolution), compare microscopes, and do calculations accurately.

Key Definitions

Magnification – how many times bigger an image is than the real object.

Resolution – the ability to distinguish two close points as separate (higher resolution = more detail).

Light vs Electron Microscopes

Feature Light Microscope Electron Microscope
Type of radiation Light Electrons
Typical resolution Lower (less detail) Higher (more detail)
Magnification Lower Higher
What it can show Whole cells and large structures Internal cell ultrastructure (e.g., membranes, ribosomes)
Living specimens? Yes (sometimes) No (specimens must be in a vacuum)

Grade 9 point: Electron microscopes have higher resolution because electrons have a much shorter wavelength than light.

Required Practical: Preparing a Slide

Method (High Mark Version)

  1. Place a drop of water on a clean slide.
  2. Use tweezers to place a thin sample onto the water (thin = clearer image).
  3. Add a drop of stain (e.g., iodine) if needed to increase contrast.
  4. Lower the coverslip carefully at an angle to avoid trapping air bubbles.
  5. Use the lowest magnification objective lens first, then increase magnification.

Common Exam Pitfalls

  • Air bubbles reduce image quality and can be mistaken for structures.
  • If the specimen is too thick, light cannot pass through clearly.
  • Start at low magnification so you can find the specimen before zooming in.

Magnification Calculations (Grade 9)

Formula:

Magnification = image size ÷ real size

You must use the same units for both sizes (convert mm ↔ µm if needed).

Unit Conversions You Must Know

  • 1 mm = 1000 µm
  • 1 µm = 0.001 mm

Worked Example 1

An image of a cell is 45 mm. The real cell is 15 µm. Find the magnification.

Step 1: Convert 45 mm to µm: 45 mm = 45,000 µm

Step 2: Magnification = 45,000 ÷ 15 = 3000×

Worked Example 2

The magnification is 400×. The image size is 20 mm. Find the real size.

Rearrange: Real size = image size ÷ magnification

Real size = 20 mm ÷ 400 = 0.05 mm = 50 µm

6-Mark Model Answers

6 marks: Compare light and electron microscopes. Light microscopes use light and have lower resolution and lower magnification, so they show fewer details. Electron microscopes use electrons and have much higher resolution because electrons have a shorter wavelength, allowing smaller structures to be distinguished. Electron microscopes can show internal ultrastructure such as membranes and ribosomes, but specimens must be in a vacuum so living cells cannot be observed. Light microscopes can sometimes view living specimens, but cannot show as much detail.
6 marks: Describe how to prepare a slide for a light microscope and how to get a clear image. A drop of water is placed onto a clean slide and a thin specimen is added using tweezers. A stain such as iodine may be added to increase contrast so structures are easier to see. A coverslip is lowered at an angle to avoid trapping air bubbles which reduce image quality. The microscope is started on low magnification to locate the specimen, then the objective lens is increased to view more detail. Focusing is adjusted to produce a sharp image, and keeping the specimen thin allows light to pass through clearly.
