Cell Transport

AQA GCSE Biology Higher — complete specification coverage of diffusion, osmosis, and active transport.

Overview

Cells must move substances across their membranes to survive. This happens by:

  • Diffusion
  • Osmosis
  • Active transport

Diffusion

Diffusion is the net movement of particles from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration.

Diffusion happens because particles are in constant random motion.

Factors Affecting the Rate of Diffusion

  • Concentration gradient – steeper gradient = faster diffusion
  • Temperature – higher temperature = more kinetic energy
  • Surface area of the membrane
  • Diffusion distance – thinner membrane = faster diffusion

Osmosis

Osmosis is the diffusion of water molecules across a partially permeable membrane.

Water moves from an area of high water concentration (dilute solution) to an area of low water concentration (concentrated solution).

Effects of Osmosis on Cells

  • Animal cells may burst in very dilute solutions.
  • Plant cells become turgid in dilute solutions.
  • In concentrated solutions, plant cells become flaccid.

Grade 9 wording: turgor pressure is created when water enters the vacuole and pushes the cytoplasm against the cell wall.

Active Transport

Active transport moves substances from a lower concentration to a higher concentration, against the concentration gradient.

This process:

  • Requires energy from respiration
  • Uses carrier proteins in the cell membrane
  • Allows cells to absorb substances even when concentrations are low

Specification example: Root hair cells absorbing mineral ions from soil.

Comparison of Transport Mechanisms

Process Direction of Movement Energy Required? What Moves?
Diffusion High → Low concentration No Particles
Osmosis High → Low water concentration No Water only
Active Transport Low → High concentration Yes Particles

6-Mark Model Answer

Explain the differences between diffusion, osmosis, and active transport. Diffusion is the net movement of particles from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration and does not require energy. Osmosis is a special type of diffusion involving only water molecules moving across a partially permeable membrane from a dilute solution to a concentrated solution. Active transport moves substances from a low concentration to a high concentration against the concentration gradient and requires energy from respiration and carrier proteins in the cell membrane.
